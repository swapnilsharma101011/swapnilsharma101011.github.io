1 PM Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojna

The PM Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojna is a flagship initiative by the Indian government aimed at providing financial support to small and marginal farmers. The best aspect of this scheme is its direct transfer of ₹6,000 annually to farmers in three installments, which enhances their liquidity and supports agricultural activities. This income support helps alleviate financial stress, promotes sustainable farming practices, and contributes to rural development. By ensuring timely and unconditional aid, the scheme empowers farmers to invest in their agricultural needs and improve their livelihoods.

2 Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maan Dhan Yojana (PM-KMY)

The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maan Dhan Yojana (PM-KMY) is a pension scheme for small and marginal farmers in India. The key benefit of PM-KMY is that it provides a guaranteed monthly pension of ₹3,000 to farmers aged 60 and above, ensuring financial security in their old age. Farmers contribute a nominal amount during their working years, and the government provides the remaining amount. This scheme aims to provide a safety net for farmers post-retirement, helping them maintain a stable income and improve their quality of life in their later years.

3 PRADHAN MANTRI FASAL BIMA YOJANA (PMFBY)

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is a crop insurance scheme designed to provide financial support to farmers in the event of crop loss due to natural calamities, pests, or diseases. The best aspect of PMFBY is its comprehensive coverage and affordability. Farmers pay a minimal premium (2% for kharif crops, 1.5% for rabi crops, and 5% for commercial crops), while the government subsidizes the rest. It offers timely compensation, ensuring that farmers receive immediate financial assistance to recover from losses, stabilize their income, and continue farming activities. The scheme aims to protect farmers from the uncertainties of agriculture.

4 Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana

Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) is an Indian government scheme aimed at enhancing the growth and development of the agricultural sector. The best aspect of RKVY is its focus on providing financial support for various agricultural activities and infrastructure development. The scheme offers grants to state governments to implement projects that boost agricultural productivity, improve farm incomes, and develop rural infrastructure. RKVY promotes innovation and efficiency by supporting initiatives such as irrigation, seed production, soil health, and farmer training. Its flexible approach allows states to tailor projects according to local needs, fostering comprehensive agricultural development.

5 Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) is an Indian government initiative aimed at promoting organic farming practices. The best aspect of PKVY is its focus on encouraging sustainable agriculture by supporting traditional, organic farming methods. It provides financial assistance to farmers for converting conventional farms into organic farms, including subsidies for organic inputs, certification, and training. The scheme enhances soil health, reduces dependence on chemical fertilizers, and improves crop quality. By promoting organic farming, PKVY helps farmers achieve higher market prices for their produce and contributes to environmental sustainability and the health of consumers.

6 OPERATIONAL GUIDELINES OF NATIONAL BAMBOO MISSION

The National Bamboo Mission (NBM) is designed to significantly enhance bamboo cultivation and the bamboo industry across India. It aims to boost bamboo production by providing financial support and subsidies for planting and developing bamboo plantations. The mission also prioritizes research and innovation to advance bamboo technology and improve cultivation techniques. It invests in building processing infrastructure to add value to bamboo products, which includes setting up bamboo clusters and enhancing market linkages. Through training programs and capacity-building initiatives, the mission empowers farmers with the necessary skills and knowledge to optimize bamboo farming. By fostering a robust bamboo sector, the NBM seeks to increase farmers' income, promote sustainable development, and create employment opportunities in rural areas.

8 Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY)

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY) aims to enhance irrigation infrastructure and water use efficiency in Indian agriculture. The scheme supports the development of micro-irrigation systems like drip and sprinkler irrigation, which help optimize water usage. It also focuses on watershed development projects to improve water conservation and management in rain-fed areas. By promoting efficient water use, PMKSY seeks to increase agricultural productivity, reduce water wastage, and ensure sustainable water resources for farming. 9 National Agriculture Market (eNAM)

National Agriculture Market (eNAM) is an initiative that integrates existing Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC) markets across India into a unified online platform. This digital marketplace facilitates the online trading of agricultural commodities, allowing farmers to access a broader market beyond their local areas. eNAM promotes transparency in transactions, which enhances price discovery and ensures fair pricing for produce. By reducing the role of intermediaries, the platform helps farmers get better prices for their crops and improves market efficiency. Additionally, eNAM supports the development of a robust agricultural marketing infrastructure, providing a more streamlined and equitable trading process for farmers.

10 Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGA)

Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA) aims to enhance livelihood security in rural areas by providing at least 100 days of wage employment per year to every rural household. The scheme guarantees employment to adults willing to do manual work, thereby creating a safety net for the unemployed. It focuses on generating sustainable assets such as roads, ponds, and irrigation facilities while improving rural infrastructure. By ensuring minimum wages and promoting rural development, MGNREGA helps uplift rural economies, reduces poverty, and improves the quality of life for rural residents. The scheme fosters self-reliance and economic stability in rural communities.

11 Kisan Karj Mafi Yojana (Uttar Pradesh)

Kisan Karj Mafi Yojana in Uttar Pradesh offers debt relief to farmers by waiving off their loans under specific conditions. This scheme is designed to alleviate the financial burden on distressed farmers who struggle with high levels of debt. By reducing or eliminating outstanding loans, the program helps prevent the forced liquidation of assets, thus enabling farmers to maintain their agricultural operations. The scheme promotes financial stability and encourages continued farming activities, supporting the overall well-being and sustainability of the farming community. It aims to improve the economic conditions of farmers and ensure their financial security.

12 Mahatma Phule Crop Insurance Scheme (Maharashtra)

Mahatma Phule Crop Insurance Scheme (Maharashtra) provides crop insurance to safeguard farmers against losses due to natural calamities like floods, droughts, pests, or diseases. It helps reduce the financial burden on farmers during times of crisis and stabilizes their income, allowing them to continue farming without severe setbacks. By offering timely support, the scheme encourages farmers to adopt better agricultural practices, thereby promoting sustained agricultural activities and long-term economic resilience in Maharashtra's farming community.

15 Kisan Credit Card (KCC)

Kisan Credit Card (KCC) is a scheme designed to provide short-term credit to farmers for essential agricultural inputs such as seeds, fertilizers, and pesticides. It offers low-interest loans, allowing farmers to access necessary funds quickly and efficiently. The KCC facilitates timely financial support, helping farmers manage their agricultural expenses and optimize their crop production. By easing the credit process and reducing the cost of borrowing, the scheme supports enhanced agricultural productivity, growth, and financial stability for farmers. It aims to improve access to timely credit, which is crucial for successful farming and sustaining agricultural activities.
